## Company Cards

Company cards represent Companies that the players build in order to produce goods and services. Each one lists the amount and type of Workers it requires to be operational, the resources it produces, and (when appropriate) the Wage that those Workers are paid.

The color of the Company indicates the **Industry** in which it belongs. There are 5 Industries in total in the game:

- **Agriculture** (green)
- **Luxury** (blue)
- **Healthcare** (red)
- **Education** (orange)
- **Media** (purple)

### Capitalist Class / Public Companies

Key features on the card:

- **Company Name** — the name at the top
- **Company Cost** — top corner (a star ★ indicates Starting Company; small diamonds indicate player count: 2 diamonds = 2-player games, 3 diamonds = 3 & 4 player games)
- **Industry** — indicated by color
- **Goods / Services Produced** — number of resources produced
- **Required Workers** — shown by meeple icons (color indicates skilled Worker in the corresponding industry)
- **Wage Levels** — Level 3 (highest), Level 2, Level 1 (lowest)
- **Machinery Production Bonus** — Machinery tokens can be placed on Companies to boost their production

### Middle Class Companies

Middle Class Companies come in 2 types: those run solely by Middle Class Workers and those with Working Class employees.

Key features:

- **Middle Class Worker Type** — the Middle Class Worker(s) required
- **Working Class Worker (Employee) Type** — optional employee slot
- **Employee Production Bonus** — bonus production from the employee
- **Employee Wage Indicator** — the Wage for the employee

### Other Types of Companies

- **Cooperative Farm** — A special type of Company that only the Working Class can build through some of its action cards. It requires 3 unskilled Workers to operate and produces 2 Food. It is considered to be a Private Company but is not placed on the board.
- **Automated Companies** — Companies with a large Machinery symbol printed on them and no Worker slots. They are considered to always be operational, do not require Workers, and are treated as having a Machinery token on them. In the Production Phase they produce without any Wage paid.

## Action Cards

Action cards allow you to perform actions on your turns. You may either play them for their effect or discard them to perform Basic Actions.

Key features:

- **Illustration** — thematic art
- **Card Title** — name of the action
- **Card Effect** — what happens when played
- **Card Requirement** — policy requirement that must be met to play for effect
- **Role Indicator** — which class the card belongs to
- **Legitimacy Change** — symbols at the bottom showing Legitimacy changes (ignore if playing with less than 4 players)

## Export Cards

Export cards show 8 possible transactions that players can make when selling goods and services to the Foreign Market. A new Export card is revealed at the beginning of each round during the Preparation Phase.

## Political Agenda Cards

Political Agenda cards (used in 4-player games only) are drawn each round by the State and indicate the Political Agenda of the current administration. The State is awarded VP at the end of each round, depending on how close the current political situation is to their card.

## Immigration Cards

Immigration cards indicate the type of Immigrants (skilled/unskilled) that come into play at the beginning of each round, during the Preparation Phase. Each card shows one Working Class Worker and one Middle Class Worker.

## Event Cards

Event cards (used in 4-player games only) show various issues that the State will have to deal with. They include:

- **Event Title** — the issue
- **Event Task** — what needs to be done
- **Possible Choices** — the Classes to choose from
- **Reward** — gained depending on the choice made
- **Penalty** — applied if No Action is taken

## Business Deal Cards

Business Deal cards allow the Capitalist Class to buy multiple goods (Food and/or Luxury) from the Foreign Market at a lower than usual price. Depending on the current Foreign Trade Policy (Policy 6), tariffs may need to be paid to the State unless those goods are sold only back to the Foreign Market.

Key features:

- **Type and amount of goods provided**
- **Main cost of goods**
- **Tariffs imposed based on Policy 6 — Foreign Trade** (paid to the State)

## Storages

Storage tiles allow you to increase the capacity of the Storages in your player board (Capitalist Class and Middle Class only). Regular Storages can be bought by the Capitalist Class or obtained through Action cards by the Middle Class and they effectively double the storage capacity on your board. There is also a special Storage — the **Free Trade Zone** — that the Capitalist Class starts the game with. Only goods (Food and Luxury) can be stored in the Free Trade Zone and they may only be sold in the Foreign Market afterwards — not the other players.

## Game Board Areas

- **Politics Table** — shows the 7 Policies and their current sections (A, B, C)
- **VP Track** — keeps track of players' points
- **Round Track** — keeps track of the current round
- **Tax Multiplier** — tracks the Tax Multiplier value used by Capitalist and Middle Class taxes
- **Vote Results** — spaces for placing cubes during elections
- **Business Deals** — where Business Deal cards are placed
- **Export** — where Export cards are placed
- **Import** — shows prices for importing goods from the Foreign Market
- **State Treasury** — where the State's money is kept
- **Private Sector** — where Companies built by the Capitalist Class and the Middle Class are placed
- **Public Services** — where resources produced by the State (Health, Education, Influence) are placed
- **Public Sector** — where Public Companies are placed
- **Unemployed Workers** — where new Workers are placed when they come into the game
- **Immigration** — where the Immigration deck is placed
- **Events** (4-Player only) — where Event cards are placed
- **State Benefits** (4-Player only) — where benefits provided by the State are placed

## Player Boards

### Working Class

- **Population Track** — records the number of Working Class Workers in the game, which determines Population and resource needs
- **Prosperity Track** — shows Prosperity level; increasing it is the main source of VP
- **Trade Unions** — slots for skilled Workers in each of the 5 industries
- **Income** — where money is kept
- **Goods & Services** — where bought resources are kept until used

### Middle Class

- **Population Track** — records the number of Middle Class Workers in the game
- **Prosperity Track** — shows Prosperity level (different scale from Working Class)
- **Storage Areas** — where produced goods and services are stored, with capacity limits and selling price indicators
- **Income** — where money is kept
- **Goods & Services** — where bought resources are kept until used

### Capitalist Class

- **Revenue** — where all new money is stored (untaxed)
- **Capital** — where money goes after taxation; VP scored based on total Capital
- **Wealth Table** — indicates VP scored based on Capital amount
- **Storage Areas** — where produced goods and services are stored, with capacity limits and selling price indicators

### State

- **Legitimacy Tracks** — 3 tracks (one per Class) showing how each Class perceives the State
- **Influence Storage** — where Personal Influence is stored
- **Food & Luxury Storage** — for goods the State may acquire during the game
